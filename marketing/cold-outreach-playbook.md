## Part 1: LinkedIn DM Sequences

### Rules for LinkedIn DMs
- Message 1 (connection request note): 300 characters max. No pitch. Be human.
- Message 2 (day 5 after connect): Add one specific observation. One soft CTA.
- Message 3 (day 12 after connect): Final touch. Short, honest, door open.
- Never send a pitch before they accept the connection request.
- Never attach anything in a DM.

## Part 2: Cold Email Sequences

### Rules for Cold Emails
- Subject lines: under 7 words, specific to their world
- Email 1: under 150 words, no pitch, lead with their pain
- Email 2: add new value — a specific result or example
- Email 3: the breakup — short, honest, no guilt trips
- Plain text format. No HTML design. No logo headers.
- One CTA per email. Always: a 30-minute call.

## Part 3: Objection Handling

### "We already have someone doing our marketing."

This is the most common early deflection. It conflates web development with marketing — which gives you an opening. Acknowledge fully, make the distinction, ask one question.

---

#### 3A. LinkedIn DM Version

> Totally makes sense — sounds like you've got that covered. Quick distinction
> though: we're dev, not marketing. We build the actual website and tools. A lot
> of clients work with us on the technical side and keep their marketing person
> for campaigns. Worth a 5-minute call to see if there's overlap?

---

#### 3B. Email Version

Subject: Re: [original subject]

Hi [First Name],

Got it — and that's great that you have someone on that already.

One small distinction: what we do is on the development and systems side, not marketing. Your marketing person tells people what to say and where to say it. We build the actual website, the booking system, the online store — the tools that the marketing then drives traffic to.

A lot of our clients work with a marketing agency and bring us in separately for the technical build. They're not the same spend.

If that distinction is relevant to your situation, I'm happy to talk for 30 minutes. If not, totally understood.

https://calendly.com/elevenclicks-info/30min

Ronak
ElevenClicks

---

#### 3C. Live Phone or Video Call Version

**What to say:**

> "That's great — I'm glad you have someone handling that. Can I ask a quick clarifying question? When you say marketing, are they also managing the actual website builds and technical development, or more the campaigns and content side?"

*[Let them answer.]*

**If they confirm it's just marketing (ads, social, email):**

> "That's what I figured. We're actually on the development side — we build the website, the app, the booking system. It's a different function. A lot of our best clients already have a marketing person, and they use us in parallel because their marketer doesn't want to deal with the technical build. Does that change anything?"

**If they say their marketing person handles the website too:**

> "Okay, helpful to know. How's that been working? I ask because sometimes when one person is doing both the campaigns and the technical site work, one of them gets the short end of the stick. Is the site where you want it to be right now?"

The goal in a live call is not to win the argument — it is to get them to say out loud what is and is not working. Once they identify a gap, they are selling themselves on the conversation.

## Part 4: 30-Day Follow-Up Cadence

### The 30-Day Schedule

| Day | Channel  | Action                                       | Time     |
|-----|----------|----------------------------------------------|----------|
| 1   | Email    | Cold intro (industry-specific)               | 10 min   |
| 2   | LinkedIn | Connection request with note                 | 3 min    |
| 5   | LinkedIn | Follow-up message (if connected)             | 3 min    |
| 6   | Email    | Value-add follow-up (reply to thread)        | 5 min    |
| 8   | LinkedIn | Engage with a recent post (passive)          | 2 min    |
| 10  | Phone    | Voicemail (optional, if number known)        | 5 min    |
| 12  | LinkedIn | Final LinkedIn message                       | 2 min    |
| 13  | Email    | Breakup email (reply to thread)              | 3 min    |
| 30  | Internal | Set re-engagement reminder                   | 1 min    |

**Total active time per prospect: ~34 minutes over 30 days.**

---

### Day-by-Day Notes

**Day 1 — First Email**
Personalize the opening line. Reference something specific about their business. If you cannot find anything specific, do not send.

**Day 2 — LinkedIn Connection Request**
Do not send the connection note and email on the same day. Treat them as two separate touchpoints — reference nothing about the email in the LinkedIn note.

**Day 5 — LinkedIn Message 2**
Only if they accepted the connection. If they haven't accepted yet, try again on day 7.

**Day 6 — Follow-Up Email**
Reply to your original email so it appears as a thread. Do not start a new email chain.

**Day 8 — LinkedIn Engagement**
Like or comment on one recent post. Not promotional. Just keeps your name visible.

**Day 10 — Voicemail (Optional)**
> "Hey [First Name], this is Ronak from ElevenClicks. I sent you a couple of emails about [specific pain point] — just wanted to put a voice to the name. Happy to chat for 30 minutes if the timing makes sense. You can book directly at elevenclicks.com or just reply to my email. Take care."

Do not call clinic owners during patient hours — early morning or after 5pm only.

**Day 12 — Final LinkedIn Message**
Keep it short. No new pitch. Leave the door open. Done on LinkedIn after this.

**Day 13 — Breakup Email**
Reply to the same thread. Subject: "Closing your file" — consistently gets above-average open rates because it is honest and unexpected.

**Day 30 — Re-Engagement Flag**
Set a 60-day reminder to re-engage with a completely new angle.

---

### Re-Engagement Rules After 30 Days

| Outcome | Wait | Re-engage approach |
|---|---|---|
| No response (all 7 touches) | 60 days | New angle, new trigger, no reference to previous |
| "Not now" or "maybe later" | 90 days | Reference previous conversation, give specific new reason |
| "Not interested" | 6 months | Only if genuinely new relevant reason exists |
| Booked call, went cold | 2 weeks | "Did you have a chance to think about what we discussed?" |
